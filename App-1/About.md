

# Background

Over the past 15 years, the United States has experienced a steady increase in vaccine 
hesitancy among parents of young children. As a result, a growing proportion of children 
are either partially vaccinated, vaccinated on delayed schedules, or exempted from vaccination 
requirements.

Several immunizations are mandated for school entry, including vaccines against tetanus, diphtheria, 
pertussis, hepatitis B, measles, mumps, rubella, and varicella. Although all U.S. states permit medical 
exemptions, they vary substantially in their allowance of non-medical exemptions, including 
philosophical and religious exemptions.

Recent shifts in public health guidance and political discourse, including movements such as MAHA, 
have contributed to evolving state-level policies on childhood immunization requirements.

In a previous analysis, Idaho was identified as having one of the highest reported rates of 
immunization exemption in 2024, reaching approximately 15%.

# Study Objective

This study examines potential associations between immunization exemption rates, political 
affiliation, and selected social determinants of health within the state of Idaho.

The primary focus is on county-level variation in:

- Socioeconomic indicators:
  - median household income
  - poverty rate
  - lack of educational attainment (capped at grade 9)
  - language isolation

- Political indicators:
  - voter registration by party

The guiding hypothesis is that higher levels of immunization resistance are associated with 
political affiliation and/or lower educational attainment (@Iasulaitis2025; @Vardavas2023). 
Additional relationships between socioeconomic indicators, such as education and income, are 
also explored (@Edwards2024).

# Data and Methods

This analysis uses county-level aggregated data for Idaho. Counties are treated as observational 
units representing population-level averages of social and political characteristics.

Due to the unavailability of county-level immunization exemption data (requested from the Idaho 
Department of Health but not yet obtained), this study relies on proxy indicators derived from social 
determinants and political composition.

Variables analyzed include:

- median household income
- poverty rate
- population density
- lack of educational attainment
- language isolation
- voter registration by political party

Standard exploratory statistical methods are applied, including correlation analysis and linear 
regression at the county level.

# Limitations

This study is ecological in design, using county-level aggregates rather than individual-level data. 
As such, findings are subject to ecological inference limitations and should not be interpreted as 
individual-level associations.

Additionally, several socioeconomic variables are inherently interrelated (e.g., median income and 
poverty rate), which may introduce multicollinearity and overlapping explanatory power.

# Future Work

Once county-level immunization exemption data for Idaho becomes available, it will be incorporated 
into the analysis to strengthen inference and enable direct assessment of the primary outcome of 
interest.
